GameStop Fiscal Year 2024 Results: Increasing Profitability and Value

For the second year in a row, despite store closures and reduced revenues, GameStop is net profitable with a year over year increase in profitability and highest equity valuation in its history

Summary
  • GameStop's bottom line has increased possibly up to 500% year over year, with net income for the year estimated at $50 M
  • GameStop continues to close stores, which is shrinking the revenue base
  • At the same time, GameStop continues to modernize and improve its business, making more effective use of a smaller network of stores
  • In terms of stockholders' equity, GameStop is now more valuable than at any time in the company's history, at nearly $5 B
Year Over Year Changes

While there is clearly still room for improvement, the financial standing of the company has improved significantly since the turnaround efforts began in 2021.

Revenue, Store Count: Decreasing

GameStop has reduced its store count every year since FY 16, a trend that has continued through GameStop's turnaround.

As the store count has decreased, so has revenue in comparable proportions.

"With respect to retail operations, we plan to continue reducing costs and focusing on profitability... This means a smaller network of stores with an expanded assortment of higher value items that fit into our trade-in model." - CEO Ryan Cohen (June 17, 2024)

GameStop revenue and store count by fiscal year
Operating Income: Still Negative

GameStop continues to report operating losses. There are numerous factors that contribute to this, including an existing network of stores that remains too large and unprofitable, inflation and economic headwinds, as well as the continuation of the trend of customers buying video games digitally rather than physically. While we've seen reductions in operating losses over the last 5 years, it demonstrates that the core business is still losing money.

GameStop operating income by fiscal year

Something else worth consideration is that GameStop is undergoing a transformation that aims to modernize the company and focus more on technology. While the specific details and goals of this transformation remain unclear with information that is publicly known, it can be assumed that the majority of expenses related to such transformation efforts would fall under operating expenses. In consideration of this, it could plausibly be the case that GameStop's core business isn't losing as much money as appears, rather, some unknown amount of these expenses are investments into a company transformation.

In either case, GameStop's legacy business is either losing money, or barely breaking even at best. Regardless, GameStop finds itself in a financially strong position that allows for operating losses for the purpose of investing in transformative efforts.

Interest Income: Carrying the Company

In 2024 (and in 2021), GameStop was able to raise money by selling shares at high market price, and now holds over $4.5 billion in cash and equivalents, which is used to generate interest income.

In fiscal year 2024, GameStop earned over $160 million in interest income, an amount that far exceeds all operating losses and other losses, ensuring net profitability for the company.

GameStop interest income by fiscal year

It is common for companies to have debt, and to pay the interest expense on that debt, and to report net interest expenses rather than net interest income. This was true of GameStop until it raised and continues to hold billions of dollars in cash. While it might be unusual for a publicly traded company to rely on interest income to offset operating losses, it is a strategy that has put GameStop into a strong financial position.

Net Income: Positive and Increasing

In fiscal year 2023, GameStop showed a return to net profitability for the first time in 6 years.

Through 2024, among other things, GameStop closed stores, reduced liabilities, and raised cash, strengthening the financial position of the company.

At the 2024 annual meeting, CEO Ryan Cohen stated that "we plan to continue reducing costs and focusing on profitability."

All told, GameStop was able to significantly improve net income year over year, in line with the CEO's statements. GameStop now shows the highest net profitability since FY 16.

GameStop net income by fiscal year
Stockholders' Equity: Higher Than Ever

By raising money from the market, GameStop has been able to pay down debts and other liabilities, and generate interest income.

GameStop now has the largest value of assets that it has had in its history, as well as the lowest amount of liabilities over the last 20 years.

In terms of stockholders' equity, this means GameStop is now more valuable than at any other time in the history of the company, at nearly $5 billion.

GameStop stockholders' equity by fiscal year
Conclusion: Stronger than Ever

GameStop has been undergoing a turnaround since the current leadership team took over in 2021, and we are now starting to see clearly some strong positive results from these turnaround efforts.

Adapting to the modern video gaming era, GameStop's legacy business of brick and mortar retail stores continues to get smaller ever year, with fewer stores and reduced revenue. Despite fewer stores and reduced revenue, the legacy business continues to be improved and modernized.

Aside from GameStop's legacy business, the company has been able to strategically take advantage of market conditions and raise cash at various times. GameStop's pile of cash and equivalents is now worth more than the entirety of its legacy business.

While GameStop's strategy may not be typical for a publicly traded company, it is a strategy that took a dying company and turned it into something more valuable than it has historically ever been.

The company continues to face challenges and still has room for improvement. What is demonstrated clearly from GameStop's fiscal year 2024 results, however, is that the company is strong and improving.
